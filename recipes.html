Pav Bhaji Recipe

  • 2 cups mixed vegetables (peas, carrots, cauliflower, potatoes, etc.)
  • 1 cup chopped onions
  • 1 cup chopped tomatoes
  • 1/2 cup chopped bell peppers
  • 3-4 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 inch ginger, grated
  • 2-3 green chilies, finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons Pav Bhaji masala
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish
  • Pav (bread rolls) for serving

Instructions:

  1. Boil the mixed vegetables until they are soft. Mash them coarsely and set aside.
  2. In a pan, heat butter and add cumin seeds. Once they splutter, add chopped onions, ginger, and garlic. Saute until onions are golden brown.
  3. Add chopped bell peppers and saute for a couple of minutes.
  4. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they are soft and oil starts separating.
  5. Add Pav Bhaji masala, red chili powder, turmeric powder, and salt. Mix well.
  6. Add the mashed vegetables and mix thoroughly. Cook for 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  7. Garnish with chopped coriander leaves.
  8. For serving, lightly toast Pav (bread rolls) with butter.
  9. Serve the Bhaji with toasted Pav, lemon wedges, and chopped onions on the side.

Golgappe Recipe

  • 1 cup semolina (sooji)
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking soda
  • 1/2 cup mashed boiled potatoes
  • 1/2 cup boiled chickpeas
  • 1/4 cup chopped onions
  • 1/4 cup chopped tomatoes
  • 1/4 cup chopped coriander leaves
  • 1 teaspoon roasted cumin powder
  • 1 teaspoon chaat masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  • Tamarind chutney and mint-coriander chutney for filling
  • Water for making the dough
  • Oil for deep frying

Instructions:

  1. Mix semolina, all-purpose flour, baking soda, and a pinch of salt in a bowl.
  2. Add water gradually to make a smooth dough. Cover and let it rest for 30 minutes.
  3. In a separate bowl, mix mashed potatoes, boiled chickpeas, chopped onions, tomatoes, coriander leaves, cumin powder, chaat masala, and red chili powder.
  4. Divide the dough into small balls. Roll each ball into a thin disc.
  5. Cut small circles using a round cutter. Deep fry them until golden brown and puffed.
  6. Make a small hole in the center of each fried disc. Fill it with the prepared potato mixture.
  7. Top with tamarind chutney and mint-coriander chutney. Serve immediately.
